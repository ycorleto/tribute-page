Isaac Newton

Scientist, Mathematician, Biblical Commentor, Professor,...

A timeline of Sir Isaac Newton's life:

  • 1642, age 0

    - Born December 25, 1642 (old calendar) in Woolsthorpe, a small village in Lincolnshire, England. Two months prior, his father Isaac Newton died."

    - In today’s Gregorian calendar, Isaac was born January 4th, 1643.

  • 1645, age 2

    - His mother Hannah Ayscough married Barnabas Smith, of 63 years of age, the minister of the church at North Witham. Isaac was left in the care of his maternal grandparents.

  • 1645-1653, ages 2-10

    - The period in which Isaac was under the care of his maternal grandparents. Isaac learned to read and write from his material grandmother and mother.

  • 1653, age 10

    - Barnabas Smith passes away. Isaac’s mother, Hannah, returned to Woolshtorpe with the three children she had with Smith.

  • 1655, age 12

    - Attends the King's boarding school in Grantham

  • 1659, age 16

    - Returns hoem to manage the family farm full time; a task he is not very sucessful with

  • 1659-1661, ages 16-18

    - Hannah’s brother, the headmaster of the Grantham school, persuades her that Isaac should prepare to enroll into a university. Isaac receives further schooling at Grantham.

  • 1661, age 18

    - Matriculates at Trinity College, Cambridge.

    - Funds his studies by taking on the job of a sizar (acting as a servant for social superior classmates or for tutors)

  • 1662, age 19

    - Draws up a list of 48 sins he committed before and then after Pentecost of this year.

    - E.g., “Using the word (God) openly”, “Threatning my father and mother Smith to burne them and the house over them”, “Wishing death and hoping it to some”, “Punching my sister”, “Fearing man above Thee”, “Using unlawful means to bring us out of distresses”, etc.

  • 1661-1665, ages 18-22

    - Isaac’s education covered the classics on Aristotelian rhetoric, logic, ethics, and physics. Isaac later begun to teach himself mathematics by studying the works by Oughtred, Viete, Wallis, and Descartes.

  • 1663, age 20

    - Becomes friends with John Wickins and then onward are roommates for the next twenty years.

  • 1665, age 22

    - Received his bachelor’s degree in April 1665.

    - A small scale of the bubonic plague strikes England. In the summer, Isaac returns home to Woolsthorpe.

  • 1665-1666, ages 22-23

    - Isaac makes his initial experimental discoveries in optics and independently developed the mathematical theory of uniform circular motion. He sets down the basic rules of differentiation and integration in a paper of October 1666. This later becomes the fundamentals of what is now called calculus.

  • 1667, age 24

    - Elected to a fellowship in Trinity College after the university had been closed due to the plague.

    - Continues his research in optics.

    - Extends his tract on calculus by incorporating new work on infinite series.

  • 1669, age 26

    - Replaces Isaac Barrow as Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Trinity upon Barrow’s recommendation.

    - Begins a more private research in the subjects of alchemy and theology.

  • 1671, age 28

    - Completes most of a treatise length account of calculus. Unfortunately, Isaac found no willing publisher to take on calculus during this time.

  • 1672, age 29

    - Submits his works in optics to the Royal Society and published in the Philosophical Transcation of the Royall Society journal.

  • 1672-1676, ages 29-33

    - Isaac’s work in optics is challenged by figures like Robert Hooke and Christiaan Huygens.

  • 1679, age 36

    - Returns to Woolshorpe to care for his dying mother who later dies on April 14th.

  • 1684-1687, ages 41-44

    - Extends his work on “De Motu Corporum in Gyrum” (“On the Motion of Bodies in Orbit”) becoming the 500 page, Principia.

    - Theory of universal gravitation: every object in the universe attracts and is attracted to every other object.

  • 1689-1690?, ages 46-47

    - During this period, Isaac represented Cambridge University in the Convention Parliament.

    - Befriends John Locke and Nicolas Fatio de Duillier.

    - Meets Christiaan Huygens in person.

    - Writes his principal treatise in alchemy, Praxis

    - Corresponds with Richard Bentley on religion.

    - Exchanges with John Locke discussing passages in the Bible on the Trinity.

  • 1696, age 53

    - Accepts the position of Warden of the Mint in the city of London

  • 1699, age 56

    - Becomes Master of the Mint in the city of London

  • 1701, age 58

    - Represents Cambridge University in Parliament for 16 months

    - Resigns his Fellowship and Lucasian Professorship from Trinity College

  • 1702, age 59

    - Designs Queen Anne’s Coronation Medal

  • 1703, age 61

    - Elected President of the Royal Society

  • 1704, age 62

    - The first English edition of Isaac’s Opticks is printed

  • 1705, age 63

    - Knighted Sir Isaac Newton by Queen Anne. This marked the first time which a scientist was given such an honor.

  • 1706, age 64

    - A latin edition of Opticks is printed

  • 1709, age 67

    - Begins work towards a second edition of Principia

  • 1713, age 71

    - A second edition of Principia is printed. Adds a ‘General Scholium’ setting out Newton’s view of the relationship between God and Creation

  • 1716, age 73

    - Compiles his theories on ancient chronology at the request of Princess Caroline of Wales. Newton asks her to keep the manuscript to herself which she does not. A Short Chronicle from the First Memory of Things in Europe, to the Conquest of Persia by Alexander the Great

  • 1717, age 74

    - A second English edition of Opticks is printed

  • 1721, age 78

    - A third English edition of Opticks is printed

  • 1722, age 79

    - Begins to suffer from bladder stones

  • 1723, age 80

    - Begins work towards a third edition of Principia

  • 1726, age 83

    - A third edition of Principia is printed

  • 1727, age 84

    - Attends his last Royal Society meeting on February 19. Suffers from a new bladder stone that night.

    - Dies on March 20, 1727 having refused the last rites.
